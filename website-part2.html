Pizza and its history

Pizza is a savory dish of Italian origin, consisting of a round, flattened base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with ingredients such as tomato sauce, cheese (typically mozzarella), and various other toppings, which is then baked at a high temperature. It is one of the most popular foods in the world. The history of pizza begins with various ancient cultures producing flatbreads with toppings. The modern pizza, however, evolved in Naples, Italy, where the poor working class would top their flatbreads with tomatoes and other simple ingredients. The iconic Pizza Margherita is attributed to Neapolitan baker Raffaele Esposito, who, in 1889, created a pizza for Queen Margherita of Savoy featuring the colors of the Italian flag: red (tomato), white (mozzarella), and green (basil). Italian immigrants brought pizza to the United States in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, where it gained immense popularity and evolved into distinct regional styles.

The Margherita pizza is a classic, traditional Neapolitan pizza. It is renowned for its simple, fresh ingredients that represent the colors of the Italian flag: red from the tomato sauce, white from the mozzarella cheese, and green from the fresh basil leaves.

Barbecue pizza typically refers to a pizza that uses a barbecue sauce base instead of traditional tomato sauce, and is topped with ingredients such as BBQ chicken, red onions, and cheese. It is a popular, flavorful dish that can be made at home or ordered from various restaurants, including local options in the Mirpur, Dhaka area.
