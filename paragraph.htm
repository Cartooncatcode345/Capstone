A paragraph is a group of sentences that work together to support a single main idea, often introduced by a topic sentence. While there is no strict rule for length, a paragraph should be long enough to fully develop its point but not so long that it becomes overwhelming. The key components of a well-structured paragraph are a clear main idea and supporting details that are logically connected and flow smoothly from one sentence to the next.

what are they

  • Main Idea: The core of a paragraph is a single, controlling idea that all sentences support. This is often stated in a topic sentence, which is frequently the first sentence of the paragraph, but can also appear elsewhere.
  • Structure: A paragraph typically includes a topic sentence, several supporting sentences that provide details, evidence, or explanations, and sometimes a concluding sentence.
  • Length: Paragraphs can vary in length. In some cases, a single sentence can form a paragraph, while in academic writing, they are often longer to allow for more thorough development of ideas.
  • Cohesion: Sentences within a paragraph must be connected and flow logically to create a cohesive unit. Using connective words can help improve this flow and readability.
  • Purpose: Paragraphs break up a text into manageable chunks, making it easier for the reader to follow and understand the writer's ideas.
